About us

Our organization was founded on the idea that art can transform not only spaces but also minds. We see every exhibition as an opportunity to rethink aesthetic boundaries and create new ways for people to experience beauty. The development of each project involves careful study, experimentation, and the pursuit of new forms of expression that reflect today’s cultural and social dynamics. We believe that true innovation is born from curiosity and openness — qualities that guide our team in every creative decision. By combining artistic vision with modern technology, we aim to build a platform where artists and audiences can share ideas, emotions, and inspiration.

Gallery

Kazimir Malevich

«Woman with a rake»

1931–1932

This painting is from the second part of Kazimir Malevich's Peasant Cycle. The artist began working on it in 1930–1931, after the first cycle was lost following the Berlin and Warsaw exhibitions of 1927.

Catalog

The catalog brings together a diverse collection of artworks that reflect both the legacy of classical traditions and the spirit of modern creativity. Each piece represents a unique perspective on form and meaning, revealing how art continues to evolve through innovation, experimentation, and the pursuit of beauty in all its expressions.

Domenico Ghirlandaio

June 2, 1448 – January 11, 1494

One of the leading Florentine artists of the Quattrocento, he founded an artistic dynasty continued by his brother David and son Ridolfo. He headed the workshop where the young Michelangelo spent a year mastering his craft. He created fresco cycles that vividly depict, in meticulous detail, the domestic lives of biblical figures (portrayed by noble citizens of Florence in period costume).
